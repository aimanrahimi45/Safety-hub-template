INTRODUCTION

As you complete your installation work, you will find various procedures at your work locations that must be followed specifically for you're the workplace conservation, health & safety for all parties.

This Handbook gives brief details of issues that may be found at the workplace. Although every effort has been made to make the handbook comprehensive, it cannot detail all the aspects or risks you may encounter in the course of your work. It is important that you use the Handbook as a reference in addition to the training you will receive from site.

Safety preservation is the responsibility of everyone! We encourage input from staffs and sub-contractors to help improve the workplace.

If you are uncertain of the correct and safe way to do the job, ask advice – Do Not Take Chances.

Remember, you are not expected to work in an unsafe manner just to get the job done.

ENVIRONMENT, HEALTH AND SAFETY POLICY

The Environment, Health & Safety (EHS) policy of Metrod, which is involved in the manufacture and marketing of copper products for electrical application, is driven by the vision to be a responsible corporate citizen with integrity, high ethical standards and in harmony with environment.

We are committed towards providing a healthier, cleaner, and greener environment by implementing effective EHS management system, guided by our "M.E.T.R.O.D" principles that include:

  • ➤ Manage our occupational and surrounding environments to be safe, healthy and clean.
  • ➤ Enhance our performance by continual improvement of management system and to achieve the set objectives and targets.
  • ➤ Train all the employee to improve competency and active participation at all levels.
  • ➤ Rightfully comply with all applicable legal and other requirements.
  • ➤ Optimise and conserve the available resources for sustainable development and prevention of pollution.
  • ➤ Deploy relevant and necessary resource for the improvement of work place ergonomics and environment.

The EHS Policy is communicated, understood, implemented and maintained throughout the organization and other interested parties.

LEGAL FRAMEWORK & COMPLIANCE

This contractor handbook is specifically developed to ensure compliance with the OSH Act 1994 (Occupational Safety and Health Act, Amendment 2022). All this requirements now applies to all places of work throughout Malaysia and introduces stringent new legal obligations for principals and contractors.

All procedures and requirements in this handbook are legally mandated under the new Act. Non-compliance with these guidelines may result in significant penalties, including fines up to RM500,000 and potential imprisonment. This handbook serves as your practical guide to meeting these legal obligations while ensuring workplace safety.

  • Complete Job Safety & Environmental Analysis (JSEA) before starting work
  • Identify all hazards and implement control measures
  • Review and update risk assessment if conditions change
  • Obtain approval from site supervisor before commencing high-risk activities

PERMIT TO WORK REQUIRED FOR:

  • Hot work (welding, cutting, grinding)
  • Working at heights above 2 meters
  • Confined space entry
  • Electrical work on live systems
  • Excavation work
  • Work near hazardous substances
NO WORK SHALL COMMENCE WITHOUT PROPER PERMITS AND RISK ASSESSMENT

SAFETY FIRST

UNACCEPTABLE BEHAVIOUR

For the safety and security and that of others, the following behaviour will not be tolerated:

  • Practical jokes and silly antics
  • Fighting or threatening to fight
  • Assaulting, threatening or interfering with other persons
  • Intoxication at work from drugs or alcohol
  • Abuse, damage or destruction of company property
  • Interfering with, removing without permission, company property or that of another person
  • Interfering, tampering or bypassing or rendering inoperative controls or guards designed to provide protection
  • Failing to follow safety procedures and practices

ALCOHOL AND DRUGS ON SITE

The possession of illegal drugs at work is strictly prohibited.

Any person who reports for work under the influence of alcohol or drugs is considered a hazard to him/herself, his/her workmates and everyone else.

SMOKING ON SITE

STRICTLY Non-Smoking zone within company premises.

CHEMICALS ON SITES

No matter how safe a chemical may appear to be, it is essential to observe the following:

  • Review the Material Safety Data Sheet attached to the generic SDS/Work Method Statement if you are unfamiliar with the product
  • Avoid contact with skin – do not splash about
  • Avoid breathing or swallowing chemical
  • Use a barrier cream if working with chemicals regularly
  • Wear prescribed personal protective equipment

All wastes shall be disposed properly and correctly upon completion of job/task.

General waste:

  • Dispose to designated general waste disposal area

Scheduled waste:

  • Collect all scheduled wastes upon completion
  • Store temporarily at designated area
  • Remove all scheduled waste from the site

COMPRESSED AIR SAFETY

Compressed air can cause serious injury including:

  • Airborne particles made airborne
  • Injuries can be caused by the air jet and by particles made airborne
  • Airborne particles can enter your eyes or abrade skin. On rare occasions compressed air can enter the blood stream through a break in the skin or through a body opening
  • An air bubble in the blood stream is known medically as an embolism (blood vessel is blocked)
  • An embolism of an artery can cause coma, paralysis or death depending upon its size, duration and location

Safety measures:

  • Ensure portable cutting equipment has appropriate extraction or there is access to a vacuum exhaust, preferably with disposable filters
  • Wear approved respirators (e.g. dust masks) when cutting or employing preparation unit
  • Do not sweep or blow dust away
  • Rotate tasks between workers wherever possible to minimise exposure
  • Persons with wood dust skin sensitivity should wear suitable personal protective equipment, e.g. overalls, gloves, glasses, goggles
  • Wash hands and any other body parts contaminated with dust before eating or smoking

DUST

On site installations often involve cutting/machining which can cause dust.

Always assess the installation requirements to limit on site cutting and machining wherever possible.

Strive to take accurate measurements to avoid the need to cut and fit on-site.

Isolate area for cutting away from other persons on site.

Ensure cutting area is well-ventilated – cut outside if possible.

Put plastic sheeting under cutting area to facilitate clean up.

CONFINED SPACE & WORKING AT HEIGHTS

CONFINED SPACE SAFETY

  • Obtain confined space permit before entry
  • Test atmosphere for oxygen, toxic gases, and flammable vapors
  • Maintain continuous ventilation
  • Station attendant outside at all times
  • Use harness and lifeline systems
  • Emergency rescue plan must be in place

WORKING AT HEIGHTS (Above 2m)

  • Use proper fall protection equipment
  • Inspect all safety equipment before use
  • Secure all tools to prevent dropping
  • Establish exclusion zones below work area
  • Weather conditions must be suitable

TOOLS & EQUIPMENT

LOCKOUT/TAGOUT (LOTO) PROCEDURES

  • Only authorized personnel may perform LOTO
  • Identify all energy sources before isolation
  • Apply personal locks and tags
  • Test equipment to verify isolation
  • Only the person who applied the lock may remove it

TOOL SAFETY

Always use the correct tool for the job ensuring that tools are in good order.

  • Inspect all tools before use
  • Power tools must have current electrical safety tags
  • Damaged tools must be removed from service immediately
  • Portable electrical tools must have RCD protection
  • Report defective equipment to supervisor

  • Site warning system
  • Escape routes
  • Assembly area in emergency

EMPLOYEE CONSULTATION

Please raise any safety concerns with site management, the site OHS Representative and/or your Supervisor.

At induction, the Principal Contractor should advise who the site Health and Safety Representative is.

FIRE PROTECTION

The risk of fire is always present, but can be reduced by keeping your work area clean.

Know where the fire extinguishers and alarms are located.

Do not put yourself at risk when handling an emergency situation – get assistance.

Only use firefighting equipment for the purpose it was intended for.

Ensure good access to fire fighting equipment is always maintained.

FIRST AID

Report all workplace injuries to your Project Supervisor.

At induction, the Principal Contractor should outline who the site first aiders are and where first aid supplies may be found.

In an emergency, do not panic. Remember:

  • Assess the area – look for danger to yourself, the injured person and any bystanders. Take action to ensure safety, or await trained rescuers but do not move the person if they are in further danger
  • Assess consciousness of the injured person. Call for help
  • Airway – clear and open airway
  • Breathing – check for breathing
  • Circulation – check for pulse

HAZARDS – IDENTIFICATION AND CONTROL

All hazards, i.e. any condition that could cause injury, illness or damage, must be reported.

Complete Shopfitters will provide you with generic JSEA/Work Method Statements which outline potential hazards for installation.

Before you start work, please complete the Site Hazard Identification form to capture site specific hazards and how these will be controlled. The completed form is to be given/faxed to your Project Supervisor.

HOUSEKEEPING & ACCESS

Poor housekeeping in the workplace puts everyone at risk from trips and falls.

All tools and equipment should be safely stored when not in use.

Doorways and walkways must always be kept clear in case of an emergency.

Ensure that temporary barriers are used as required to isolate work areas from the public/passersby.

HYGIENE

Often sickness can be avoided by keeping tidy and clean, maintaining hygiene standards, e.g washing hands frequently after using the toilet and before eating.

Ensure that any body part or clothing is kept clean – serious infections can result from a minor scratch if not cleaned.

INCIDENT REPORTING

All incidents must be recorded on the Incident Report Form provided to you. The completed form is to be provided to your Project Supervisor.

This reporting procedure is very important because:

  • Neglect, minor injuries can become more serious
  • The incident should be investigated to allow the identification of controls so that the incident cause may be eliminated
  • Serious injuries/incidents require reporting to state authorities – please contact your WorkSafe Authority for reporting requirements

LADDERS/ELEVATED WORK PLATFORMS

Before considering using a ladder, give consideration to whether:

  • the items can be done on the ground with extension tools
  • the construction or repair of the item or part of it can be done on the ground
  • the item being accessed can be relocated to ground level to eliminate the need to work at height temporarily or permanently
  • an elevating work platform such as a scissor lift or cherry picker. A fixed or mobile work platform can be used
  • scaffolding or use a mobile scaffold can be used
  • a work positioning system such as a travel restraint or industrial rope access system can be used
  • a step platform can be used, and
  • fixed stairs or steps can be installed that comply with relevant Australian Standards and building codes

If none of the above options are not reasonably practicable for the given situation, then it may be acceptable to use a ladder provided it can be used safely.

When using ladders:

  • Always check the ladder before use
  • Where possible, place the ladder clear of walkways and traffic areas
  • Position ladder in a safe manner with both feet on firm level footing
  • Ensure sufficient slope – 1 measure out to 4 measures up
  • Secure the ladder at least 1 metre above the landing place
  • Secure ladder or have it held by a workmate
  • Don't use a metal ladder near electricity

MANUAL HANDLING

  • Never attempt to lift beyond your capacity. Test the object's weight before handling – if too heavy, get a mechanical aid or some assistance
  • Plan the lift – know where you want to lift to and that the access way is clear
  • Stand close to the load with feet solidly placed and far enough apart for stability
  • Grip the load firmly – away from sharp edges or slippery areas
  • Move your body close to the load – do not lift at arms length
  • Bend your knees, lift by straightening legs and keeping back straight
  • If doing a team lift, ensure that clear instructions are issued by only one person and there is a pre-arranged plan

PERSONAL PROTECTIVE EQUIPMENT

  • Eye safety equipment must be worn on site if there is risk of flying particles or chemicals entering the eyes
  • Use barrier cream if required to protect hands from chemical contact
  • Always wear gloves when handling sharp edged/pointed material
  • If working outdoors presents a risk of sunburn or skin cancer – a wide brimmed hat and a long sleeve shirt
  • Wear a mask in dusty environment or if chemicals need protection
  • When working in an area where there is risk of head injury by a vehicle or if falling objects, you will require hearing protection
  • If it is necessary to raise your voice to be heard by someone less than 1 metre away, you will require hearing protection

SIGNS ON SITE

Pay attention to all site warning signs, which are there for your protection.

Do not remove, alter, shifting or destruction of any sign without authorisation may put people at risk and is therefore forbidden.

VEHICLE SAFETY

  • No one shall operate any vehicle, plant, mobile or other equipment unless they are properly trained and authorised to do so – licences must be held where required
  • Vehicles must be parked safely and securely, not posing a hazard to other vehicles
  • Obey speed limit signs – do not speed on site
  • Look out for pedestrians everywhere and be aware in heavy vehicles at reversal times
  • Loads on vehicles must be secure and safe
  • Wear seatbelts fitted to all vehicles
  • At no time should an additional person be allowed to ride on any vehicle, or any other purpose
  • Use of forklifts as work platforms or for lifting people from platforms is strictly prohibited work practice
  • Keep your distance from site vehicles and be aware of the hazard they present
